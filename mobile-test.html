Our Story

January 28, 2014
Jason and Ann met through Coffee Meets Bagel - an online dating site that finds prospective partners by searching your Facebook friends and looking for friends of friends who have also signed up. While Jason and Ann had never met, they had several dance friends that knew each other.

February 1st, 2014
After Ann's date for the evening fell through, she texted Jason lamenting her bad luck and suggesting an impromptu date. Jason took a gamble and offered dinner at Chef Geoff's. Jason knew she could be the one when they ended up talking past closing time and were the last table to leave.

February 15th, 2014
Jason invited Ann over for a movie but was too embarrassed about his bathroom to let her use it. So he sent her to the bathroom downstairs. Unfortunately, she slipped down the stairs and ended up with three stitches on her elbow...

March 14th, 2014
While they met through their dance friends, Jason and Ann actually shared a deeper passion for music. Jason got box seats for Man of La Mancha @ the Modell Perorming Arts Center and thus began their habit of attending musicals and concerts.

April 5th, 2014
Jason had a two-week business trip to Jacksonville, FL and invited Ann to visit over the weekend in between. Having recently declared their relationship "official", their first trip revealed their shared passion for exploring.

April 14th, 2014
Ann had always wanted to see an opera at the Met so Jason arranged a road trip to NYC. On the way home, Ann had a major bout of food poisoning and they stopped overnight at a random hotel in New Jersey. Ann knew he could be the one after Jason cared for her in time of sickness...

The Non-Proposal

August 15th, 2015

Before the proposal was the "non-proposal". Jason and Ann had a summer trip planned to the Pacific Northwest. The plan was to fly into Washington State and drive down the Pacific Coast Highway to San Francisco stopping at various destinations along the way with Redwood National and State Parks as the highlight. Sadly, there was a slight wrinkle in the plan as Jason had to attend a sudden funeral before the trip. While attending the funeral, Jason realized life was short and precious. While he wasn't ready to propose, he wanted to be prepared for when the time came. So he sold some stock so he would have the funds on hand needed to buy an engagement ring. One week later, Jason and Ann were roaming around the dense California redwood forests. As the day wound down, they decided to explore Fern Canyon before watching the sunset at Gold Bluffs Beach. They emerged from the canyon just before sunset and the beach was completely empty with not a soul in sight.

As Ann stood on the beach staring into the sunset, Jason recognized the romantic intimacy of the moment. He wanted to say something meaningful and profound when suddenly he had an epiphany! He walked up behind Ann, tapped her on the shoulder and told her that he had something important to share. After attending the funeral the previous week, Jason realized that life is short and there wasn't any sense in waiting. He knew that he loved Ann and wanted to spend their lives together. As tears welled up in Ann's eyes, Jason proceeded to pull his phone out of his pocket and proudly showed Ann how he had saved up enough money to buy her a ring someday! An expectant Ann asked "...and?" to which Jason innocently replied "oh - that was it" ignorant to the fact that he had missed the perfect opportunity to propose. Ann would disappointedly have to wait while Jason obliviously patted himself on the back for "successfully" saying something profound and taking advantage of the moment.

The Proposal

February 13th, 2016

With the cat out of the bag after the "non-proposal", the pressure was on for Jason to find a surprising moment to actually propose. With Ann being a teacher, the only reasonable time to get married and enjoy a honeymoon was summer. After spending months looking for the right ring together, Jason knew he had to speed up the process lest they have to wait until summer 2017 to be married. With the actual ring search at a roadblock, Jason set out to find a sentimentally appropriate placeholder ring. Given his debacle at the redwoods, he thought it would be poetic that the placeholder ring would be made from California redwood. He found an artisan who crafted rings from California redwood and could use a custom gem as the center stone. With the redwood representing the end of their Pacific Northwest trip, Jason acquired a small Helenite stone - a gem made from the ash of Mount St. Helen to represent the start of their memorable trip. And thus, Jason had a placeholder ring to propose with. Now he just needed a way to surprise Ann.

Ann was attending the 2016 ACDA Divisional Conference in Boston over Valentine's Day weekend. With Jason's company holding their annual meetings the following week, by the time Ann would be getting back from Boston, he'd already be on the way to Orlando. And so Jason hatched a plan to surprise Ann in Boston and enlisted the help Ann's friends and her mother - fellow choral directors who would also be in Boston for the same conference. Jason arrived in Boston the afternoon of the last day of the conference and proceeded to decorate the hotel room. Ann's friends sneakily convinced Ann to take a side trip to another hotel room on the way to dinner. Ann's mother opened the door and a surprised Ann found Jason waiting in the center of the room where he formally proposed.

Traditions

Invites

Background

Unlike Western traditions, the color white is avoided for Chinese weddings as that color is typically used at funerals. Instead red is the predominant color used in weddings as it represents good fortune and joy. The cover of the invite depicts a golden dragon and phoenix which is a symbol of harmonious balance in marriage between the husband (dragon) and the wife (phoenix).

Double Happiness

Background

The symbol is actually the Chinese character '喜' (meaning joy or happiness) written twice. For this reason, it is commonly associated with weddings and appears frequently in decorations. It is often stylized to fit the particular setting or theme. For example, the symbol here has been stylized to fit a circular pattern to fit a sign. The symbol on our website is stylized with a heart replacing the bottom of the character.

Attire

Background

As a blended wedding, Ann will be wearing both a traditional white wedding gown and a traditional red cheongsam (a.k.a qi pao) at various points during the day. As a result, in addition to avoiding wearing white dresses on the day of the wedding, please avoid wearing red dresses.

The rest of the wedding party will be dressed up in formal attire so feel free to dress up as well! However, you are welcome to dress to your comfort. Note that if you were invited to the private ceremony, it will be outdoors on a hot summer day.

Chinese Wedding Banquet

Background

We are extremely excited to share a traditional Chinese wedding banquet with you. Most of you probably think of Chinese food as wonton soup, lo mein, and shrimp fried rice. We will be eating authentic Chinese dishes and if you've never had real Chinese food, they you're in for a treat! A Chinese banquet typically includes at least 8 dishes. The dishes are served family style and a set of dishes may arrive in rapid fashion. It's important to pace yourself because many of the best dishes are later in the sequence. Several of the dishes have specific meaning associated with them. The menu has not yet been set as it depends on the number of guests attending - but you can look forward to at least a chicken dish, a duck dish, a lobster dish, and a noodle dish. Please let Jason or Ann know about any food restrictions - we will do our best to accommodate you!

Wedding Jam

Background

Most wedding favors are somewhat frivolous trinkets that people order in bulk and seem very impersonal. Sure it may have the name of the bride and groom printed on it, but really - are you really going to hold onto that magnet or paperweight? We wanted something that was deeply personal and that you would enjoy. As wedding favors, we made you jam! We started by picking fruit together, cutting and processing it, combining all the raw ingredients, and canning it into 8 oz jars. We'll have five different flavors to choose from and there will be a set sampler jars at each table for you to try before you choose which flavor to take home with you. Of course there'll only be so many of each flavor at the table so you'll have to negotiate with your neighbors!

Travel Info

Flying Here

Reagan National Airport (DCA)

This airport is the most convenient for travelers who do not wish to rent a car. The airport is on the Washington Metro Yellow Line which, via transfer, can be used to travel to the hotel where transportation options exist for the ceremony and reception.

Baltimore-Washington Thurgood Marshall International Airport (BWI)

This airport is the most convenient for travelers who plan on renting a car. There is a shuttle from the airport to the nearby rental car facility where a variety of rental car companies are housed. Traffic to Wheaton from BWI is considerably less than from either Reagan or Dulles. BWI is also a hub for Southwest Airlines.

Washington Dulles International Airport (IAD)

This airport is the most convenient for travelers arriving from international destinations. Public transportation options are less than ideal from Dulles and traffic in around the DC Beltway area can be heavy during Friday rush hour (which extends from approximately 3pm - 7:30pm).
